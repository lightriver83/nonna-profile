# Nonna — Live Presentation Script

**Target:** 10–15 minute in-class delivery in English.
**Audience:** classmates + teacher in an English-language course.
**Goal:** present a credible, emotional, textbook-correct company profile, so the other groups understand the quality and structure expected for their own.

> Use this script as a **guide, not a jail**. Speak it — don't read it.
> Breathe on every paragraph break. Pause 2 seconds after every "." in **bold**.

---

## How to use this script

| Column          | What it means                                                                      |
|-----------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| `[SLIDE]`       | Approximate moment of the webpage to scroll to.                                    |
| `[BODY]`        | Gesture / posture cue. 55 % of the message.                                        |
| `[VOICE]`       | Pace, volume, tone cue. 38 % of the message.                                       |
| **bold**        | Stress the word — small emphasis.                                                  |
| **BOLD CAPS**   | Pause two full seconds after this word. Hold the silence. Don't fill it.          |
| *italic*        | Slow the pace here; these are the "sticky" lines the audience should remember.    |

## 0 · Before you start (30 sec of silence — really)

`[BODY]` Stand feet shoulder-width apart, weight centred, shoulders down. Laptop open. Webpage loaded.
`[BODY]` Look at the room. Find three faces — left, centre, right. Smile. Do **not** start talking.
`[VOICE]` Take one deep diaphragmatic breath. In through the nose, out through the mouth. Then begin.

---

## 1 · Opening hook — *the tortellini* (≈ 60 sec)

`[SLIDE]` Hero section. Scroll paused at "Preserve wisdom, one recipe at a time."

> In December 2022, a woman named Carla died in a small house just outside Bologna.
>
> She was 84. She was a grandmother. She was, to her family, the keeper of Christmas. **BOLD CAPS**
>
> Two months later, her granddaughter tried to recreate her tortellini recipe for the family dinner. *It wasn't the same.*
>
> It wasn't close.
>
> Because that recipe had never been written down. It lived only in Carla's hands. And Carla's hands were gone. **BOLD CAPS**

`[BODY]` Put one hand, palm open, flat in front of you at chest height when you say "her hands".
`[VOICE]` Drop volume by 20 % on the last sentence. Let silence hang.

> Good morning. I'm here to introduce you to the company that was born from that silence.

`[BODY]` Smile. Open posture. Energy up.

> Its name is **Nonna**.

---

## 2 · What we do — mission (≈ 60 sec)

`[SLIDE]` Scroll to the Mission section: *"We turn every grandparent into a living cookbook."*

> Nonna is a cultural-tech company based in Bologna.
>
> **Our mission is simple.** We turn every grandparent into a *living cookbook*. **BOLD CAPS**
>
> Not a PDF. Not an app folder. A living cookbook. Video, voice, handwriting, hands. The way a recipe is *actually* taught — by someone who loves you, in the kitchen where they cooked for you. **BOLD CAPS**

`[VOICE]` Slow the pace on *"the kitchen where they cooked for you."* This is a sticky line.
`[BODY]` Use your hands to mime something small and precise — the shape of a tortellino, the turn of a spoon.

---

## 3 · Vision — where we're going (≈ 45 sec)

`[SLIDE]` Scroll to Vision.

> Our vision is bigger than a product.
>
> *A world where no family recipe dies with its creator.* **BOLD CAPS**
>
> One sentence. One goal. One generation from now, we want the phrase *"Grandma's recipe died with her"* to sound as old-fashioned as *"we only have this photo on paper."*

`[VOICE]` Drop to almost a whisper on the italic line, then come back up.

---

## 4 · How it works — three steps (≈ 2 min)

`[SLIDE]` Scroll to the "How it works" section.

> Everything we do fits into **three steps**. Three. Not ten.
>
> **Step one — Record.** *[beat]* A family member presses one button. A guided overlay captures every step of the recipe in cinema-grade video, with automatic multilingual transcription. No tripods. No jargon. No editing. **Grandparents don't need another app. They need to be seen.** **BOLD CAPS**
>
> **Step two — Preserve.** *[beat]* Each recipe becomes a permanent entry in your private *Living Cookbook*: the video, a handwritten card, a voice note, the ingredient list. It's passed down as an *heirloom*, not a file.
>
> **Step three — Taste.** *[beat]* One click ships the artisan ingredient kit to relatives anywhere in the world. Nonna's recipe, in Nonna's exact proportions, rebuilt in your kitchen on a Sunday. **And that — the kit — is where we make our revenue.** **BOLD CAPS**

`[BODY]` Count the three steps on your fingers. Make it visible from the back row.
`[VOICE]` Same rhythm on all three: verb, short explanation, sticky sentence. The audience will start to feel the pattern — that's on purpose.

---

## 5 · The numbers (≈ 60 sec)

`[SLIDE]` Scroll to the "By the numbers" section. Let the counters run.

> Numbers.
>
> Because beautiful stories without numbers are poems, not businesses.
>
> Since our launch in March 2025:
>
> - **37 412 recipes** preserved.
> - **14 countries** served.
> - **612 regional cuisines** mapped — more than any food archive in Europe.
> - **93 % family satisfaction**, with a Net Promoter Score of 78 — audited by Deloitte Italy, Q4 2025.
>
> **Revenue** is 4.2 million euros in annual recurring terms. Gross margin, 62 %. Burn rate — *zero*, since Q3 2025. **BOLD CAPS**

`[VOICE]` Read the numbers **slightly slower** than natural speech, letting each one land. Numbers told fast sound like bragging. Numbers told slow sound like facts.

---

## 6 · The team (≈ 60 sec)

`[SLIDE]` Scroll to Team.

> Three founders. One grandmother.
>
> **Maria Conti** — CEO. Carla's granddaughter. Former brand strategist at Slow Food. She builds the **why**.
>
> **Luca Ferretti** — CTO. Former food-tech engineer at Barilla. MIT Sloan Fellow 2021. He builds the **how**.
>
> **Sofia Amaru** — Chief Creative Officer. Documentary filmmaker — Vice, Netflix. She builds the **what** you see.
>
> And then there is Carla. She isn't on the cap table. *She's on the wall.*

`[BODY]` Look up and slightly to the side when you say the last line — as if the wall were behind you.
`[VOICE]` Warm. Not theatrical.

---

## 7 · Why now (≈ 60 sec)

`[SLIDE]` Scroll to "Why now".

> A question you should be asking: *why Nonna, why now?*
>
> In Europe, **34 %** of people under 25 cannot name three recipes their grandparents cooked.
>
> In the United States, that number is **51 %**.
>
> One grandparent passes away **every 90 seconds** in Italy alone.
>
> So, every year we wait — an entire generation of oral tradition walks quietly off the stage. **BOLD CAPS**
>
> We built Nonna because *technology finally caught up with tenderness*.

`[VOICE]` Bring volume down on "walks quietly off the stage." This is the emotional peak. Don't oversell it.

---

## 8 · Trust — proof we're real (≈ 30 sec)

`[SLIDE]` Scroll to Trust.

> We're backed by United Ventures, endorsed by Slow Food, documented by UNESCO, and covered by the Financial Times, Monocle, and Kinfolk.
>
> The FT called us *"the most quietly radical Italian startup of the decade."* **BOLD CAPS**

`[BODY]` Keep hands relaxed. Don't point at the logos — let the page speak.

---

## 9 · Close — circle back to the tortellini (≈ 60 sec)

`[SLIDE]` Scroll to CTA.

> We started with a tortellini that could not be rebuilt.
>
> We said: **never again**. **BOLD CAPS**
>
> Every family in this room — *yes, yours, and yours, and yours* — has a recipe that is one funeral away from being lost.
>
> Our job is to stop the clock.
>
> **Preserve wisdom,** *one recipe at a time.* **BOLD CAPS**
>
> Thank you.

`[BODY]` Hand open, palm up, towards the audience on the final line. Hold eye contact. Do not speak again until someone asks a question.
`[VOICE]` The last line is almost a whisper. Let it land. **Do not rush to "Any questions?"**

---

## 10 · Q&A readiness — what you will be asked

Prepare a 1–2 sentence answer for each.

1. **"Is Nonna real?"** — *"No. Nonna is a fictional company we built as a textbook-correct example of a company profile. Every design choice is meant to teach you how to build yours."*
2. **"Where's the revenue?"** — kits + premium subscriptions (family plan €9/mo). Kits carry 62 % gross margin; subs are the retention engine.
3. **"Isn't this just an app?"** — it's a *memory company*. The app is the camera. The kit is the product. The recipe is the brand.
4. **"Competitors?"** — no direct. Adjacent: Storyworth (written only), Remento (generic memoir), Eatwith (live classes, no preservation). None preserve *the hands*.
5. **"Biggest risk?"** — onboarding grandparents. We mitigate with a guided grandchild-led flow. The grandchild is the user; the grandparent is the subject.

---

## 11 · The rules you just applied (say this to the class at the very end)

> Before we open Q&A — a word to my classmates.
>
> Everything you just heard applied the rules from our course file:
>
> - A **story first**, not a product. (Starbucks / Tesla opening.)
> - A **mission in one sentence**; a **vision in one sentence**. No essays.
> - **Three key ideas** max, **repeated three times**.
> - The **7 C's**: Clear, Correct, Complete, Concrete, Concise, Consideration, Courteous.
> - **55 % body language, 38 % voice, 7 % words** — so we worked on all three.
> - **Strong action verbs** — preserve, turn, record, rescue, rebuild — not "make" or "help".
> - **Stats and facts** to make the brand believable.
> - And we remembered the painful arithmetic of communication: *intent 100 %, said 70 %, heard 40 %, remembered 10 %*.
>
> So we aimed for the 10 % that matters:
>
> ***Preserve wisdom, one recipe at a time.***
